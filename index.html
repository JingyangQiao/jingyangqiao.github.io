Abstract

Multimodal Continual Instruction Tuning (MCIT) is adopted to continually instruct Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) to follow human intent in sequential tasks.

It meets two major challenges: catastrophic forgetting (inferior performance on seen task) and zero-shot degradation (inferior performance on unseen task).

In this paper, we propose a method called Multimodal Large Language Continual Assistant (LLaCA) to address the challenges.

Based on exponential movement average (EMA), we construct a set of equations, aiming to equip our method with excellent anti-forgetting ability. By solving the equation set, we obtain a theoretical derivation for the dynamic adaption weight of EMA.

Through extensive experiments on LLaVA-1.5 in continual visual question answering benchmark, compared with baseline, our approach not only highly improves anti-forgetting ability (from 22.67 to 2.68) but also significantly promotes zero-shot performance (from 6.68 to 47.55).
